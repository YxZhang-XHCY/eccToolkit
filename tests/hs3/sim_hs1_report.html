🔬 eccDNA Type Definitions:
Unique-locus eccDNAs (UeccDNAs) derive from a single contiguous genomic region and have one unambiguous origin locus.
Multi-locus eccDNAs (MeccDNAs) are composed predominantly of repetitive sequences, resulting in alignments to multiple genomic locations.
Chimeric eccDNAs (CeccDNAs) contain two or more segments from non-contiguous genomic loci, indicating a fusion of distant sequences.
